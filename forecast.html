Key Forecast Finding

Western/Growth suburbs are projected to outperform on percentage growth (up ~35% by 2031) even though they start from the lowest base. This reflects affordability-driven demand as Sydney buyers look further south and the Dapto–Horsley corridor benefits from infrastructure investment. Coastal suburbs remain the most expensive in absolute terms but show more moderate growth (~28%) as prices are already elevated.

Methodology

How the forecast was built

Model
ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average) implemented in SAS Viya. Parameters selected via AIC minimisation. Each area type modelled independently to capture different market dynamics.
Data Inputs
Median house prices (CoreLogic/YIP), weekly rents (NSW DCJ Rent and Sales Report), net migration data (ABS), population projections (NSW DPE), and housing supply pipeline (DA approvals).
Limitations
Forecasts assume no major policy shocks (e.g. negative gearing changes, foreign investment restrictions), stable interest rate environment from 2026, and continuation of current migration trends. Confidence intervals widen significantly beyond 2027.

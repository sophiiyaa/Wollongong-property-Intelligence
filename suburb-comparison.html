Area Type Analysis

Which areas cost the most — and why?

Suburbs were grouped into six area types based on their geographic character and proximity to key amenities. Each group was then compared by median house price.

Median House Price by Area Type

Coastal suburbs lead by a clear margin. Median figures use the middle value within each group, making them resistant to outliers like East Corrimal's $1.84M.

Key Finding

Coastal suburbs are the most expensive area type at $1.57M median — despite being the furthest from UOW on average (11.3km). This suggests beach proximity is a stronger price driver than commute access to the university.

K-Means Clustering — Python / scikit-learn

Five market segments, identified by the data

K-means clustering (k=5) was applied to price, distance to UOW, distance to station, and distance to beach — all standardised. The algorithm independently grouped suburbs into five meaningful segments.

Coastal Premium
Avg $1.63M · Beach 0.5km
Austinmer, Thirroul, Coledale, Bulli, East Corrimal
Beach proximity is the defining characteristic — price premium holds even when UOW and station access are poor.
UOW-Adjacent Hill
Avg $1.18M · UOW 2.5km
Gwynneville, Keiraville, Mount Ousley, Figtree, Balgownie
Strong long-term rental candidate — steady student and staff demand, away from beach tourism volatility.
Train-Line Mid-Price
Avg $1.19M · Station 0.8km
Wollongong, Fairy Meadow, Corrimal, Towradgi, Woonona, Port Kembla, Windang
Station proximity keeps prices elevated. Consistent commuter demand makes these reliable long-term rental markets.
Affordable Outer
Avg $887K · UOW 10.9km
Dapto, Horsley, Koonawarra, Unanderra, Warrawong, Berkeley
Highest rental yield (Dapto 4.2%). Growth-corridor profile — lower entry price, stronger income return relative to price.
Isolated Commuter
$1.48M · UOW 20km · Beach 8km
Helensburgh (standalone cluster)
Helensburgh sits alone — high price despite being furthest from both UOW and the beach. Its proximity to Sydney (train to Central ~1hr) and semi-rural appeal drive a separate pricing dynamic disconnected from the rest of Wollongong.
Clustering Insight

The algorithm merged Western/Growth and Industrial/Southern into a single "Affordable Outer" cluster — statistically, suburbs like Dapto and Warrawong are price-twins despite different manual labels. It also pulled East Corrimal out of the "Northern" group into Coastal Premium, independently confirming it as an outlier we'd already spotted driving up the Northern group's average.

Methodology

How this was built

Data sources, tools, and honest limitations.

Data Sources
Median house prices from CoreLogic via YourInvestmentPropertyMag (single consistent source — mixing Domain, REA and other sites produced $300–450/wk discrepancies for the same suburb). Distance figures estimated from known Illawarra geography and verified selectively via Google Maps.
Clustering Method
K-means clustering in Python (scikit-learn) using four features: median house price, distance to UOW, distance to nearest train station, and distance to beach. Features were standardised before clustering (StandardScaler) since price is on a different scale to distances. k=5 was chosen to compare against the five manual area types.
Limitations
Unit price and rental data could not be sourced consistently across all suburbs — suburb-level vacancy rate is paywalled (SQM Research). North Wollongong excluded from clustering due to missing price data. Distances are estimated, not survey-measured. Inner/CBD has only one suburb (Wollongong itself) so its "median" is a single data point.
